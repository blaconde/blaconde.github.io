Project Case

FCR Report

Power BI report designed to analyze customer contact sequences and evaluate first contact resolution logic through a broader time-window approach.

Power BI DAX Process Analysis Customer Service Analytics

Project Overview

This report was designed to support the analysis of customer contacts in the context of First Contact Resolution. Instead of treating each interaction as an isolated event, the solution was built to evaluate whether a given contact was truly resolved at first touch or followed by additional customer activity.

The report helps identify repeated contact patterns, supports process monitoring, and gives business users a more realistic view of customer journeys and operational effectiveness.

Core Analytical Approach

Depending on the business need, I recommend analyzing activity within at least a +28 and -28 day window around the first contact. This means looking both backwards and forwards from the initial interaction rather than focusing on a single point in time.

Looking backwards helps determine whether the issue had already appeared before, while looking forwards shows whether the customer had to come back again after the supposed resolution. Without this wider context, FCR can easily be overstated.

Why the +/-28 Day Window Matters

  • It provides context for whether the contact was truly the first one in the case
  • It helps identify repeated issues and follow-up customer activity
  • It reduces the risk of oversimplifying the customer journey
  • It supports more reliable operational and quality analysis
  • It creates a stronger basis for evaluating resolution effectiveness

What This Report Can Support

  • Analysis of repeated customer contact patterns
  • Monitoring of first contact resolution methodology
  • Segmentation by contact type, channel, or process area
  • Operational reporting for managers and process owners
  • Identifying areas where customer handling may require improvement

My Approach

  • Translating process logic into report methodology
  • Building analysis around the customer journey, not just single records
  • Designing reports that are understandable for business users
  • Combining analytical depth with practical reporting structure
  • Using Power BI to support both overview and detailed exploration

Why It Matters

FCR is often treated as a simple performance metric, but in practice it depends heavily on how customer activity is interpreted over time. A well-designed report makes this logic more transparent and helps teams avoid misleading conclusions.

This project reflects my approach to analytics: combining business understanding, process awareness, and reporting design to produce insights that are useful in real operational work.
